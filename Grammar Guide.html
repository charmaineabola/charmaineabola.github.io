Grammar

Tok Pisin has a simplified grammar compared to English. It lacks some English grammatical features such as verb conjugation for tense and person. Instead, it uses auxiliary words or markers to indicate tense and aspect.

Word Order

The word order in Tok Pisin is Subject-Verb-Object (SVO), similar to English. However, it's flexible, and the focus of a sentence can be changed by altering the word order.

Pronunciation

Tok Pisin pronunciation generally follows English rules, but with some differences. For example, the "r" sound is often pronounced like an "l" sound.
