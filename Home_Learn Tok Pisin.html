What is Tok Pisin?

Tok Pisin is a creole language spoken in Papua New Guinea. It originated as a pidgin language, which developed during the late 19th and early 20th centuries when various linguistic groups, including indigenous languages and English, interacted in the region.

Where is Papua New Guinea?

Papua New Guinea is located in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, occupying the eastern half of the island of New Guinea and numerous offshore islands. It is situated to the north of Australia, separated by the Torres Strait. To the west of Papua New Guinea lies Indonesia's Papua province, and to the east are the Solomon Islands. The country's capital, Port Moresby, is located on its southeastern coast. Papua New Guinea is part of the Melanesian region of Oceania.
